North Wind SQL Analysis
Business Questions
1.  Customer Diversity Analysis: What is the customer diversity? How globally distributed is the customer base?
What: List of unique countries where customers are located.
Why: The marketing team wants to understand how globally distributed the customer base is.
Skill: DISTINCT
Query: 
SELECT DISTINCT country AS customer_country 
FROM Customers;
Result: The customer base is distributed across 91 different countries.

2. Missing Data Audit
What: All customers who do not have a region assigned.  
Why: Incomplete customer data can affect communication and logistics.
Skill: NULL Handling
Query: 
SELECT CustomerID, CompanyName, Country 
FROM Customers 
WHERE Region IS NULL;
Result: There are 60 customers who have not been an assigned a region. This is 7.23% of the customer pool.

3. Order Volume Overview: What is the total business activity? 
What: Total number of orders placed.
Why: Management wants a quick snapshot of total business activity. 
Skill: Aggregate + Alias
Query: 
SELECT DISTINCT COUNT(CustomerID) AS total_customers,
COUNT(OrderID) AS total_orders
FROM Orders;
Result: There are 830 customers and orders in total each.

4. Revenue Calculation: What is the total revenue?
What: Total revenue using the 'Order Details' table.
Why: Finance wants to estimate the total revenue generated. 
Skill: Aggregate + Business Logic
Query: 
SELECT SUM(UnitPrice * Quantity * (1-discount)) 
AS total_revenue 
FROM 'Order Details';
Result: The total revenue for the organisation  is 1, 265, 793.04.

5. Product Performance by Category: How does each category perform?
What: Total number of products in each category. 
Why: The product team wants to understand how each category performs.
Skill: GROUP BY
Query: 
SELECT CategoryID, COUNT(ProductID) AS product_count 
FROM Products GROUP BY CategoryID ORDER BY CategoryID ASC;
Result: The category with the highest variety is category 3 (Confections), with 13 products, while category 7 (Produce) has the lowest, with just 5 products.

6. High-Value Customers: Which customers bring in significant revenue?
What: Customers who have placed more than 10 orders. 
Why: The sales team wants to identify customers who bring significant revenue. 
Skill: GROUP BY + HAVING
Query: 
i. SELECT CustomerID, COUNT(*) AS total_orders
FROM Orders  
GROUP BY CustomerID
HAVING total_orders > 10
ORDER BY total_orders DESC;

ii. SELECT COUNT(*) AS high_order_customer_count
FROM (SELECT CustomerID
      FROM Orders
      GROUP BY CustomerID
      HAVING COUNT(OrderID) > 10) AS eligible_customers;
Result: The top 5 customers include SAVEA at 31 orders, ERNSH at 30 orders, QUICK at 28 orders, HUNGO at 19 orders, and FOLKO at 19 orders.
In total, 28 customers placed more than 10 orders.

7. Average Order Value by Customer: What is the (average) cost per customer?
What: Average freight cost per customer.
Why: To understand customer spending behaviour. 
Skill: Aggregate + GROUP BY
Query: 
SELECT CustomerID, AVG(Freight) AS avg_freight_cost
FROM Orders
GROUP BY CustomerID
ORDER BY avg_freight_cost ASC;
Result: not sure how to interpret the result other than the range of average freight cost: Min-3.25, Max-215.60

8. Multiple-Product Suppliers: Who are the key suppliers?
What: Suppliers who supply more than 5 products. 
Why: Procurement wants to identify key suppliers. 
Skill: GROUP BY + HAVING
Query: 
SELECT S.SupplierID, Count(P.ProductID) AS supply_count
FROM Suppliers S
LEFT join Products P
ON S.SupplierID = P.ProductID                         
GROUP BY S.SupplierID
HAVING supply_count > 5;
Result: None of the suppliers provide more than 1 product.

9.  Countries with High Customer Base: What are the strong markets?
What: Countries that have more than 5 customers. 
Why: The expansion team wants to focus on strong markets. 
Skill: DISTINCT + GROUP BY + HAVING
Query: 
SELECT DISTINCT Country AS Customer_country, COUNT(CustomerID) AS Customer_count
FROM Customers
GROUP BY Customer_country
HAVING customer_count > 5
ORDER BY Customer_count DESC;
Result: USA - 13, Germany - 11, France - 11, Brazil - 9, UK - 7

10. Orders Without Shipment 
What: Total number of orders that have not been shipped yet. 
Why: Operations team needs to track delayed or unshipped orders. 
Skill: NULL + Business Insight
Query: 
SELECT COUNT(OrderID) AS pending_shipments
FROM Orders
WHERE ShippedDate IS NULL;
Result: There are 21 pending shipments
